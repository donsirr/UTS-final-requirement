What material possessions do you value the most?
Among my material possessions, I value my laptop and notebook the most. My laptop is more than just a device—it’s my gateway to learning, creating, and connecting with others. It’s where I write code, draft essays, and explore ideas that matter to me. My notebook, on the other hand, is my sanctuary for reflection. It holds my thoughts, sketches, and plans, serving as a tangible record of my growth and aspirations. These items symbolize my commitment to curiosity, creativity, and self-improvement, and I’d feel lost without them.
How would you describe yourself on social media?
On social media, I present myself as curious, thoughtful, and funny. My posts often revolve around technology, memes, and personal growth. I share posts that spark conversations, highlight projects I’m working on, and occasionally post about my hobbies, like coding. I try to strike a balance between showcasing my professional interests and my personal life, though I’m mindful of oversharing.
Do your social media profiles authentically represent who you are in real life? Why or why not?
My social media profiles are a curated version of myself, not a complete representation. While they reflect my interests and values, they don’t capture the full complexity of my personality or the nuances of my daily life. For example, I rarely post about moments of self-doubt or failure, even though these are significant parts of my experience. I also tend to highlight my achievements more than my struggles, which can create an impression of effortless success. That said, I do strive for authenticity by sharing content that genuinely resonates with me, rather than chasing trends or likes.
In what ways do you curate or filter your identity online?
I curate my online identity by selecting content that aligns with the image I want to project—someone who is engaged, ambitious, and positive. I avoid posting about controversial topics unless I feel I can contribute constructively, and I’m cautious about sharing personal details that could be misinterpreted. I also filter out moments of vulnerability or imperfection, not because I’m ashamed of them, but because I prefer to process those experiences privately. This curation isn’t about deception but about intentionality—I want my online presence to reflect the best version of myself, even if it’s not the whole version.
What challenges or pressures do you experience when interacting online (e.g., comparison, validation, cancel culture)?
One of the biggest challenges I face online is the pressure to appear perfect or productive. It’s easy to compare myself to others’ highlight reels, feeling like I’m not achieving enough or living up to some unspoken standard. I also feel the weight of validation-seeking, whether it’s through likes, comments, or followers, even though I know these metrics don’t define my worth. Additionally, the fear of saying something that could be misconstrued or lead to backlash sometimes makes me hesitant to share my thoughts openly.
What guiding principle or personal rule do you follow when engaging with others on social media?
My guiding principle is to engage with kindness, curiosity, and integrity. I try to listen more than I speak, ask questions rather than assume, and respond thoughtfully rather than reactively. I also remind myself that behind every screen is a real person with their own struggles and perspectives, which helps me approach interactions with empathy.
How can you personally improve your online presence to align better with your true self?
To better align my online presence with my true self, I can start by sharing more vulnerably and authentically. This doesn’t mean oversharing, but it does mean being honest about my challenges and imperfections, not just my successes. I can also reduce my focus on metrics like likes or followers, reminding myself that my value isn’t tied to external validation. Lastly, I can use social media as a tool for connection rather than comparison, actively seeking out conversations and communities that inspire and uplift me, rather than leaving me feeling inadequate. By making these changes, I hope to create an online presence that feels more genuine and reflective of who I am—both the polished and the imperfect parts.
