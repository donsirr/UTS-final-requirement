How would you describe your political self? What political values and beliefs do you consider central to your identity, and why?
I would describe my political self as progressive and pragmatic, rooted in values of equality, justice, and compassion. I believe in policies that promote social and economic equity, protect human rights, and address systemic inequalities. Central to my identity is the conviction that government should serve as a force for good, ensuring that everyone has access to basic needs like healthcare, education, and opportunity. I also prioritize environmental sustainability, recognizing that our actions today will shape the world for future generations. These beliefs stem from my upbringing, where I was taught to value fairness and empathy, as well as my experiences witnessing the impact of inequality on individuals and communities.
Which societal institution (school, church, peers, media) has significantly influenced your political beliefs? Provide an example of how these institutions shaped your political views.
The institution that has most significantly influenced my political beliefs is school. In high school, my history classes and school comelec exposed me to the principles of democracy. A turning point was a class debate on healthcare reform, where I researched the disparities in access to medical care. This experience opened my eyes to the ways systemic issues affect people’s lives and solidified my belief in the importance of policies that address these gaps. My teachers also encouraged critical thinking, pushing me to question assumptions and consider multiple perspectives, which shaped my approach to politics.
Reflecting on your political socialization, have your political beliefs changed over time? What experiences or factors prompted these changes?
Yes, my political beliefs have evolved, particularly as I’ve gained more life experience and exposure to diverse viewpoints. For example, I used to hold a more idealistic view of politics, believing that solutions were straightforward if only people had the will to implement them. I realized that change often requires compromise and incremental steps, rather than all-or-nothing approaches. This shift has made me more appreciative of the nuances of political issues and the importance of collaboration across ideological lines.
How would you define your spiritual self? What beliefs or practices give meaning and purpose to your life?
I define my spiritual self as secular yet deeply reflective, grounded in a belief in the inherent goodness of people and the interconnectedness of all life. While I follow Catholicism, I find meaning and purpose in practices like mindfulness, gratitude, and acts of kindness. I believe in living with intention, striving to leave the world better than I found it, and finding beauty in the ordinary moments of life. My spirituality is less about dogma and more about cultivating inner peace and outer compassion.
What role does spirituality or religion play in your personal identity and decision-making process? Share a specific experience when your spiritual values guided your choices.
Spirituality plays a significant role in my identity and decision-making by providing a moral compass and a sense of perspective. For example, during a difficult decision about whether to pursue a part-time job that would require relocating, I took time to reflect on my values and priorities. My spiritual practice of mindfulness helped me recognize that while the opportunity was appealing, it would come at the cost of my relationships and well-being. Ultimately, I chose to stay, prioritizing balance and connection over ambition. This decision felt aligned with my deeper sense of purpose.
Which influences (family, religious community, school, personal experiences) most strongly shaped your spiritual identity? Provide an example of how this influence affected your spiritual growth.
My spiritual identity has been most strongly shaped by personal experiences and nature. Growing up, family trips instilled in me a sense of awe and reverence for the natural world. These experiences taught me to find peace and clarity in solitude and to see myself as part of something larger than my individual existence. A specific moment that deepened this connection was a solo trip during a stressful period in college. Being surrounded by the quiet beauty of nature helped me regain perspective and reminded me of the resilience of life. This experience reinforced my belief in the healing power of nature and its role in my spiritual practice.
How do your spiritual beliefs help you cope with life's challenges? Describe a situation when your spiritual values provided you comfort, resilience, or guidance.
My spiritual beliefs provide me with resilience, gratitude, and a sense of purpose during difficult times. For instance, when I faced rejection from a program I had set my heart on, I felt disappointed and unsure of my path. Turning to my practice of mindfulness and journaling, I reflected on the lessons the experience offered—patience, adaptability, and the importance of not defining my worth by external outcomes. This process helped me reframe the rejection as an opportunity for growth rather than a failure. My spiritual values also reminded me to be grateful for the support of friends and family, which gave me the strength to move forward with hope.
