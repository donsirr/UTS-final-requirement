Which people, groups, or social institutions have had a significant impact on how you see yourself? How have these interactions shaped your understanding of your identity?
Several people, groups, and social institutions have significantly shaped how I see myself, each contributing to my understanding of my identity in unique ways.

How do you think others perceive you? What qualities, behaviors, or characteristics do you believe people associate with you?
I believe others perceive me as curious and driven, though this likely varies depending on the context of our relationship. Here’s how I think different groups might describe me.

Peers and classmates probably see me as collaborative and reliable. I’m often the one organizing meetings or helping troubleshoot code during projects. My tendency to ask questions and seek input might also make me appear curious or even slightly indecisive at times, as I like to consider multiple perspectives before forming an opinion.

My friends likely see me as loyal and empathetic, someone who listens actively and tries to support them through challenges. My humor and willingness to try new things might also make me appear adventurous and fun-loving.

To my family, I’m probably responsible but sometimes too hard on myself. They appreciate my dedication to my studies and goals but often remind me to slow down and enjoy the process.

However, I’m aware that there’s a gap between how I’d like to be perceived and how I might actually come across. For example, while I strive to be confident, my self-doubt sometimes manifests as hesitation or over-explanation, which could be misinterpreted as uncertainty. Similarly, my drive to be helpful can sometimes make me appear overly accommodating, as I struggle to say no to requests.

To gain a more objective view of how others perceive me, I asked three people from different areas of my life for their honest feedback: a close friend and a classmate. Their insights were both affirming and surprising, offering a clearer picture of how I’m seen by others.

My classmate said I’m “super reliable and great at explaining complex ideas, but sometimes overthink things.” He appreciated how I’m always willing to help with assignments or group projects, but he also mentioned that I can be hesitant to make quick decisions, which he finds a bit frustrating at times. Comparing their perceptions to my assumptions, I found both alignment and gaps. I was right in thinking that others see me as curious, reliable, and empathetic, but I was surprised by how noticeable my self-doubt and tendency to overcommit are to others. For example, I didn’t realize how often I downplay my achievements or how my hesitation in decision-making can be perceived as indecisiveness.

This task was an eye-opening. It confirmed that while I’m on the right track in many ways, there are areas where my internal struggles are more visible than I thought.
